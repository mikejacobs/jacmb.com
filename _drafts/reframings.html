<Business> is good because it creates jobs.

Jobs are seen as good in our society because they give people money to afford necessities and pay for streaming television. From a neoliberal capitalist perspective, this is a positive in so far as it incentives people to participate in our economy both through selling their labor and in buying the results of other people's labor. In fact, this is their definition of "freedom".

The problem with this story is it doesn't account for the brutality of withholding the means of survival in a system that may not even be able to provide a job for someone, and accessiblity of different jobs varies wildly based on the class and wealth you were born into. Monopolistic enterprises tend to concentrate wealth over time creating a coersive environment that limits the choices of working people in how they participate in the economy.
