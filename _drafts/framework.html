Thought Technology

In the same way that all the man-made objects around us were dreamed up by someone, then designed, constructed, and transported to us, so were our ideas. Our worldviews are an overlapping mess of frames, a stew of stories that helps us make sense of the world with ingredients collected from parents, teachers, politicians, media, and the billboard that caught you at just the right moment.

A frame is an angle, and the world looks different from behind each one, making situations and activities slippery and subjective.✶✶ From the angle of quantum physics, even solid ground isn't solid. "Everything we call real is made of things that cannot be regarded as real.” – Niels Bohr Though they become more solid and certain to us when they're shared by others, that can't be the only criteria for an ideology of frames.

Shared frames make the fabric of our society and the walls of the invisible structures that govern our lives. They can also be contradictory, or fanciful, or due for reconsideration. And it's worth noting that they're always incomplete because raw reality is full of hyperobjects our dinky brains are underequipped to comprehend.✶✶ If ever there was a sentiment that gets at my non-religious spirituality, it's this. We'll forever be blindfolded guessing at the elephant in front of us.

To add even more wrinkles, society is constantly trying to modify our frames or slyly slip another one on the pile. This underlines the importance of understanding how framing works, especially when it's used as a tool for manipulation.

Framing Pitfalls

Manipulative Framing

Often, we talk about manipulative framing as if it only applies to sales and politics―thinly veiled attempts to get us to buy goods or politicians. While it's important to know that the beer from the ad won't make you interesting, that isn't life-changing for most of us.

Manipulative framing is so pervasive that, without a proper defense, we often aren't aware that it's even happening. A secret, mind-altering spell. And because framing is a rhetorical device that shapes ideas, if it's successfully received, the frame is self-reproducing and spreads to others. By acting and communicating within them en masse, we reshape the world to fit our frames, sometimes at great cost to those that are unjustly on the wrong side of the shades.

This leads us to my interest in the larger, ambient frames―where the rubber of ideas meets the road of reality. The lenses we unknowingly wear everyday that color the world around us and bound our sense of what is possible. The bowl, table, and room in which we consume the story stew mentioned above.

Defensive Reframing

Thankfully, we don't have to be passive in the framing process. We have the power to reframe as a defense against this rhetorical device. There are questions we can ask ourselves to get at framing when it may not be apparent. A counter spell, or a spell revealer at least. I'm certainly not immune to its effects, but, I have found that with practice, it's possible to position these questions as filters in our information intake process.

Examples of questions useful in reframing:

  • How does this person benefit from telling me this?
  • Does this statement only apply to a limited group?
  • Does this statement present the whole situation or just part?
  • Why is this information being shown to me?
  • Who benefits from me acting in a certain way?
  • Who stands to gain from the options presented or excluded?
  • Who stands to gain from my work?
  • Will anything change if I do something different?
  • Who is in power in this situation?
  • Who is powerless in this situation?
  • Why is someone resisting a change?
  • Are there key words that may be misleading?
  • At what level in a process do I feel empowered to act?

This is a route to a different reality. Feeling stuck? Here's your map. But, there's one more thing you'll need if you hope to get unstuck. And to be honest, it's more difficult than the reframing mentioned above. As with any kind of adventure, you need to be ok with being changed. You need to be ok with being wrong and realizing you have wronged others. We all have, however indirectly. Openness to this takes courage. There is a real vulnerability in this kind of exploration. You need to start asking yourself these questions (and others like them) to pick apart the rotten bits of your foundational worldview. Unschool yourself of the detrimental wahtnot. You're going to feel lost. But, I am writing this to tell you of the wonderful and loving sense of belonging on the other side of that journey. The alternative is living with the world as it's handed to you, which we already know is a mixture of truths and manipulations.

I won't lie. From here the path is difficult. You will find things about yourself that may not sit well. How have you perpetuated the unjustness of our society? How have you been manipulated to do so? And some of these things may not be immediately changable. Your survival takes priority over any kind of ideological change. This is the compromised state we find ourselves in. But, this process of recognizing wrong and putting it in context is the way forward. Realize that you're doing the work. I have cultivated a deep current of anger at the many realizations of these manipulations and the sad reality is that I can only do so much to right the wrongs. The key is to direct that anger somewhere positive and productive. Otherwise it eats away at you. If you have personally done wrong, do what you can to correct it and ensure you don't do it again.
