It's fascinating how societal norms can transend more appropriate local practices. Living in the desert has led me to appreciate water in a different way than I had in the south. Where does it come from? How much do I use? What do I use it for? Am I wasting it?

While I would argue that one-time-using our water anywhere is a waste of time, energy, money... and water. Pull water out of the ground;In Tucson, we transport it in aquaducts hundreds of miles and pump it into the ground first. transport it to our homes; use it; it either sinks back into earth or it is then pumped back to a wastewater station to be treated. It's downright boneheaded, in a place like the dry, crunchy, southwestern deserts of America, to

From that angle, the way we do bathing is odd. Pipe in clean water, bathe in it, then pipe the used water away. Separately, we water our plants from the same source and it drains into the ground. This is considered normal everywhere in the United States. In a place where water is scarce, it really should not be normal.
