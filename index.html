What I'm Doing Now

Embracing the small / weird / wild web

In the rush to the new crop of corporate-owned social media sites, I've chosen a different path. Instead, I'm gleefully riding the resurgence in personal internet (as opposed to the big box sites we all flocked to for quite a while) and simple HTML sites made by individuals. Though "internet sovereignty"** An internet system in which the people who produce, distribute, and consume internet also control the mechanisms and policies of internet production and distribution. (This is the wikipedia definition for food sovereignty with the word "internet" replacing instances of "food") is a better description of the ethos I'm seeing and seeking.

The ongoing redesign of this website was a step in that direction. As was joining Mastodon — where I am finding more aligned minds and sense of community than I ever did on Twitter etc.

Growing a distributed farm

Along with a lovely group of pals, I am in the process of creating a mutual aid organization. In the simplest of terms, the goal is to grow food with each other, for each other and others that need it. That means growing food in gardens across many locations in the Tucson Metro Area, connecting those that have access to land with those that do not. We help each other grow on the land and we all share in the abundance. Think mutual aid victory gardens with land-, labor-, skill-, and harvest-sharing. In Tucson? Give me a shout.

About
